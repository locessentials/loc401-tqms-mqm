# Loc401: Translation Quality Management Systems based on MQM

Welcome to Loc401! This course introduces translation producers to contemporary Translation Quality Management Systems (TQMS) with a focus on the Multidimensional Quality Metrics (MQM) framework. Participants will explore standards-based approaches to translation quality evaluation and management based on international standards from the ASTM F43 technical committee on Language Services and Products.

The course combines theoretical foundations with practical application, teaching participants how to implement quality evaluation workflows that move beyond traditional sentence-by-sentence evaluation. Through self-directed exercises, participants will develop skills in error identification, categorization, and annotation according to the MQM Typology. Exercises cover inter-rater agreement and improvement plans based on quantitative analysis. Participants will also learn about annotator performance metrics like exact matching, F1 scores, and Kappa coefficients. 

Special emphasis is placed on evaluating translations as complete documents rather than isolated segments. The course also explores how high-quality annotated data can improve machine translation outputs and automatic post-editing systems in the age of generative AI.

## Learning Outcomes

Upon completing the Translation Quality Management Systems course, participants will be able to:

1. **Interpret and apply** ASTM F43 standards and differentiate between MQM error typology and holistic document-level evaluation (correspondence and readability ratings) to critically assess their advantages and limitations.
2. **Identify and annotate** translation errors using an MQM typology, performing contextual evaluations of whole documents rather than isolated segments.
3. **Calculate and interpret** annotator agreement metrics—including exact matching, F1 scores, and Kappa coefficients—to analyze performance data and develop targeted improvement plans.
4. **Design quality management workflows** that integrate translation specifications, structured evaluation, and annotation guidelines for both human and machine translation processes.
5. **Leverage quality-annotated data** to improve AI-driven translation systems and adapt quality management approaches across different content types and professional contexts.

## Who Should Take This Course

This course is designed for:

- Translators and editors seeking to formalize their approach to translation quality evaluation
- Translation project managers responsible for overseeing quality workflows
- Localization engineers and language leads working with annotation tools and quality metrics
- Language service providers looking to align their quality processes with international standards
- AI and machine translation teams seeking to generate high-quality annotated training data
- Anyone involved in designing or implementing translation quality management systems

### Prerequisites

No prior experience with quality management frameworks is required. Participants should have a working familiarity with translation or localization processes, either as a practitioner or manager. Course materials are available in U.S. English and Mexican Spanish. A willingness to engage with quantitative concepts, such as scoring metrics and inter-rater agreement, will be helpful.
