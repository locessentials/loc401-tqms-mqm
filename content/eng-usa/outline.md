# Course Outline

## Introduction: Translation Quality Management Systems Based on MQM

## Lesson 1: Standards-Based Approach to Translation Quality

This lesson introduces ASTM F2575-25 and its two key concepts: specifications and translation labels. Specifications transform subjective quality expectations into objective, measurable requirements, while translation labels distinguish Unverified from Professionally Verified translations based on whether a qualified professional has taken responsibility for the final product. The lesson uses the Tranquality structured specifications framework to walk through the parameters worth defining before beginning a translation project.

## Lesson 2: MQM Error Typology & Holistic Quality

This lesson presents a customized MQM error typology with seventeen error types across six categories, along with guidance on how to apply them. It expands on the standard MQM framework by introducing quality markers to recognize particularly well-handled translation challenges, and levels of impact to replace traditional severity levels. The lesson concludes with a holistic rating system that scores translations on correspondence and readability to determine overall effectiveness.

## Lesson 3: Translation Quality Management Workflows

This lesson introduces a Translation Quality Management System (TQMS) for training evaluator teams to assess translation quality consistently. It addresses the central challenge of inter-rater reliability and walks through a scaffolded sequence of training and evaluation projects designed to bring teams into alignment. Once evaluators reach the desired agreement threshold, their annotations can be used to train new evaluators, provide translator feedback, and fine-tune LLM-based quality evaluation systems.

## Lesson 4: Error Annotation Environment

This lesson compares traditional translation environment tools (TEnTs) with professional annotation platforms, examining the structural limitations of sentence-level CAT workflows for quality evaluation. It introduces Label Studio as an open-source annotation environment suited to MQM-based error annotation, and walks through how to configure and use it for structured, exportable quality evaluation.
